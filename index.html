E-Fraud

Online Fraud




  • What is E-fraud?


    Cybercrime also referred to as fraud is the use of a computer to steal, alter, or use electronic data for improper purposes. The Computer Fraud and Abuse Act, which places computer-related crimes within federal jurisdiction, specifically forbids computer fraud.





    • Types of E-Frauds-



    1) Phishing

    2) Data Breach

    3) Malware

    4)Ransomware

    5)Business email compromise (BEC)

    And many more!



      Phishing

      The tricking of victims into disclosing personal information, login information, and financial details through email and online messaging platforms.



      Data Breach

      Removing of sensitive, confidential, or protected information from a trusted site and bringing it to a less reliable one. This includes users' and organisations' data being taken.



      Malware

      The use of malicious software to take personal and sensitive data, harm or disable users' devices.



      Ransomware

      A kind of virus that restricts user access to important data and then demands payment with the promise of regaining access. Typically, phishing attacks are used to spread ransomware.



      Business email compromise (BEC)

      A sophisticated attack aimed at companies that often accept wire transfers. Through the use of social engineering tactics, it hacks legitimate email accounts to make unlawful payments.
